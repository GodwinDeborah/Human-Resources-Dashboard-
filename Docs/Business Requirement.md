# Human Resource Dashboard | Business Requirement Document (BRD)

## 1. Project Overview

The Human Resource Dashboard is designed to provide comprehensive workforce analytics that support strategic HR decision-making. The dashboard enables HR managers and business leaders to monitor workforce trends, analyze employee demographics, evaluate compensation patterns, and access detailed employee records through interactive visualizations.

The solution consists of two primary dashboards:

* **Summary Dashboard** – Provides high-level insights into workforce composition, employee trends, demographics, and income analysis.
* **Employee Records Dashboard** – Offers detailed employee-level information with advanced filtering and exploration capabilities.

# 2. Summary Dashboard

The Summary Dashboard should be divided into three analytical sections:

* Overview
* Demographics
* Income Analysis
  
## 2.1 Overview Section

The Overview section should provide a high-level snapshot of key HR metrics and workforce distribution.

### Requirements
* Display the total number of:

  * Hired employees
  * Active employees
  * Terminated employees

* Visualize employee hiring and termination trends over time.

* Present workforce distribution across:

  * Departments
  * Job titles

* Compare employee counts between:

  * Headquarters (HQ)
  * Branch locations
    *(New York serves as the company headquarters.)*

* Display employee geographic distribution by:

  * City
  * State

### Expected Business Insights

* Workforce growth trends
* Departmental staffing distribution
* Employee turnover patterns
* Regional workforce concentration

## 2.2 Demographics Section

The Demographics section should provide insights into workforce composition.

### Requirements

* Present the gender distribution across the organization.

* Visualize employee distribution by:

  * Age groups
  * Education levels

* Display the total number of employees within each:

  * Age group
  * Education category

* Analyze the relationship between:

  * Educational background
  * Employee performance ratings

### Expected Business Insights

* Workforce diversity analysis
* Age and education distribution trends
* Educational impact on employee performance
* Demographic composition of the organization

## 2.3 Income Analysis Section

The Income Analysis section should focus on compensation trends and salary distribution across different employee groups.

### Requirements

* Compare salaries across education levels for both genders to identify:

  * Salary disparities
  * Compensation trends
  * Potential pay gaps

* Analyze the relationship between employee age and salary across departments.

### Expected Business Insights

* Gender-based salary comparison
* Salary progression by education level
* Departmental compensation trends
* Relationship between age, experience, and income

# 3. Employee Records Dashboard

The Employee Records Dashboard should provide a detailed and interactive employee directory for operational analysis.

### Requirements

* Display a comprehensive employee table containing:

  * Employee ID
  * Employee name
  * Department
  * Job title
  * Gender
  * Age
  * Education level
  * Salary
  * Employment status

* Enable users to filter employee records dynamically.
* Support detailed employee exploration and drill-down analysis.

### Expected Business Insights

* Employee-level workforce tracking
* Department-specific employee analysis
* Quick access to detailed HR records
* Flexible workforce exploration

# 4. Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful if users can:

* Monitor workforce metrics efficiently
* Identify employee and salary trends
* Analyze demographic patterns
* Access detailed employee records easily
* Gain actionable HR insights through interactive dashboards
