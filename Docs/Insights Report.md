# Human Resource Dashboard | Insights Report

## Overview

This dashboard analyzes workforce trends, employee demographics, compensation patterns, and organizational distribution using HR employee data. The analysis highlights key patterns across hiring activity, workforce composition, education levels, salary distribution, and employee performance.

# Key Insights

## 1. Workforce Growth Trend Remains Positive

The dataset contains:

* 8,950 hired employees
* 7,984 active employees
* 966 terminated employees

Hiring activity exceeds employee terminations across most observed periods, indicating overall workforce growth.

## 2. Operations Represents the Largest Department

Operations maintains the highest employee count, followed by Sales, Customer Service, and IT.

This distribution suggests a workforce structure strongly centered around operational and customer-facing functions.

## 3. Employee Distribution is Concentrated at Headquarters

Approximately 70% of employees are associated with headquarters locations, while 30% are distributed across branch offices.

The workforce structure indicates a highly centralized organizational presence.

## 4. Employee Presence is Strongest in Selected States

The highest employee concentrations are located in:

* New York
* Ohio
* Pennsylvania
* Illinois

## 5. Gender Representation Remains Relatively Balanced

Workforce distribution is approximately:

* 54% male
* 46% female

The dataset reflects a relatively balanced gender composition at the organizational level.

## 6. Mid-Career Professionals Represent the Largest Workforce Segment

The largest employee populations fall within:

* 25–34 years
* 35–44 years

This indicates that the workforce is primarily composed of mid-career professionals.

## 7. Bachelor’s Degree Holders Form the Largest Education Segment

Employees with Bachelor’s degrees represent the largest educational category within the dataset.

Advanced degree holders make up a smaller but more specialized segment of the workforce.

## 8. Higher Education Levels Align with Stronger Performance Ratings

Employees with Master’s and PhD qualifications appear more frequently within higher performance rating categories.

The analysis suggests a potential relationship between educational attainment and employee performance.

## 9. Salaries Increase Alongside Educational Attainment

Employees with advanced degrees are more concentrated within higher salary ranges.

Salary distribution indicates a positive relationship between education level and compensation.

## 10. Salary Differences Between Genders are Visible Across Some Education Levels

Salary comparisons reveal noticeable differences between genders across several educational categories.

Additional analysis would be required to determine whether these differences are influenced by role type, tenure, seniority, or other workforce factors.

## 11. Managerial and Technical Roles Occupy Higher Salary Ranges

Roles such as:

* IT Manager
* Finance Manager
* Operations Manager

rank among the highest-paid positions within the dataset.

Leadership and specialized technical roles consistently align with higher compensation levels.

## 12. Salary Shows a Positive Relationship with Age

The Age vs Salary analysis indicates that compensation generally increases alongside employee age.

This trend may reflect the influence of experience, tenure, and seniority on salary progression.

# Conclusion

The Human Resource Dashboard provides an interactive overview of workforce composition, demographic trends, and compensation patterns within the dataset. The analysis highlights key relationships across education, salary, department distribution, and workforce demographics while identifying opportunities for deeper HR analysis and workforce planning.
